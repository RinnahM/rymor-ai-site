The Problem

A 23-truck HVAC and plumbing company was losing 4–5 emergency jobs a month to slow after-hours response. Calls went to voicemail, callbacks took 20 minutes, and customers called a competitor instead. At $1,200 average per emergency ticket, that's nearly $60,000 a year lost — not to bad service, but to slow response.

What I Built

A 24-node autonomous pipeline that fires the moment a call ends, triggered by a webhook straight from the voice agent. An idempotency guard hashes the incoming call data and checks it against a Supabase registry, so the same request can never create two jobs. The transcript goes to an AI model that scores urgency and extracts required technician certifications, then a Haversine distance calculation ranks every available technician by real proximity, cross-checked against the Jobber CRM for availability and skill. A draft job is created directly in Jobber, a Slack alert hits the dispatcher's phone with one Approve button, and once confirmed, a Gmail confirmation goes out to the caller automatically. Under 60 seconds, around the clock.
